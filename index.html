A Small Guide to Indoor and Outdoor Rock Climbing
in Las Vegas

In the rock climbing scene, Las Vegas is considered be an iconic spot attracting a large amount of climbers every year. Vegas is home to many developed areas all of which offer various kinds of scenery and styles of climbing.

Climbing Gyms

First and foremost, Las Vegas is home to approximately four distinct climbing gyms, all with varying faciliites and focuses. If the valley is facing extreme weather conditions, you could consider some of these indoor options as a viable substitue.

  • The Pad Climbing
  • The Refuge Climbing and Fitness
  • Red Rock Climbing Center
  • Nevada Climbing Center
The Pad Climbing

Boasting a wide variety of climbing and workout facilities, the Pad Climbing offers an ample amount of bouldering with a subsequent amount of rope options. Red Rocks

Red Rocks is an iconic climbing destination with much to offer. This sandstone paradise has a lot to offer from from big-wall Trad routes to the hardest of boulders. There are also lots of area within Red Rocks to explore to fit every climbing fans needs. Consider some of the following areas if you're planning on visiting Red Rocks.

  • Calico Basin Area
  • Scenic Loop Drive
  • Oak Creek
  • Black Velvet Trail
  • And many more areas!
Patrick Fellure Climbing "Plumbers Crack" in the Kraft Boulders Area

Red Rock Canyons typical climbing season runs from September and can run as late as early May, depending on the weather conditions. Climbers will typically avoid the summer months as temperatures can soar into the low 100s. During the aforementioned season, temperatures in the 40 - 70 degree range can be expected, allowing for comfortable climbing conditions. Some important things to not while climbing at Red Rocks, however, is that human waste has been a large problem affecting the area, so when planning your trip, be sure to account for any and all instances. To learn more about all the climbs Red Rock Canyon has to offer, checkout Mountain Project for an extensive database containing information on routes, approaches, climbing beta and much more!

Mount Charleston

Mount Charleston is located 45 minutes outside of the Las Vegas Valley and is the perfect destination for Sport Climbing fanatics. The Limestone in this area is very diverse in its features which leaves lots of different areas to explore. Consider some of theses areas while planning your trip to Mount Charleston.

  • Walla Walla Wash
  • White Pine
  • Route 158
  • The Imagination Wall
  • And many more!
"Mary Jane Cliff" in Mount Charleston

Mount Charleston's climbing season typically ranges from late May to August. Charleston is most popular during the off season of Red Rock Canyon, as during the Summer months temperatures in the Charleston area remain in a comfortable range compared to the scorching summer temperatures of the Las Vegas Valley. This area is most widely regarding for its limestone sport climbing with numerous amounts of already established crags. However, sport climbing isn't the only style of climbing it has to offer, as there are also ample amount of options for bouldering and trad climbing. Be sure to check out Mountain Project for an extensive database of everything Mount Charleston has to offer!
